# Sources & References

## Books

### Folklore Collections
- Afanasyev, A. *Russian Fairy Tales*. Classic collection of Russian folklore featuring house spirits including Kikimora.
- Ivanits, L. (1989). *Russian Folk Belief*. M.E. Sharpe. Comprehensive study of Russian folk beliefs and household spirits.
- Warner, E. (2002). *Russian Myths*. University of Texas Press. Modern compilation of Russian mythological figures.

### Slavic Mythology
- Dixon-Kennedy, M. (1998). *Encyclopedia of Russian & Slavic Myth and Legend*. ABC-CLIO.
- Bailey, J. & Ivanova, T. (1998). *An Anthology of Russian Folk Epics*. M.E. Sharpe.
- Johns, A. (2004). *Baba Yaga: The Ambiguous Mother and Witch of the Russian Folktale*. Peter Lang Publishing.

### Comparative Studies
- Ralston, W.R.S. (1872). *The Songs of the Russian People*. Ellis & Green. Historical collection of Russian folklore.
- Rose, C. (2001). *Giants, Monsters, and Dragons: An Encyclopedia of Folklore, Legend, and Myth*. W.W. Norton & Company.

## Academic Papers & Research

- Hubbs, J. (1988). "Mother Russia: The Feminine Myth in Russian Culture." Indiana University Press.
- Ryan, W.F. (1999). *The Bathhouse at Midnight: An Historical Survey of Magic and Divination in Russia*. Pennsylvania State University Press.
- Haney, J. (1999). "The Afanas'ev Collection and Russian Folklore Studies." *Slavic and East European Journal*, 43(2).
